Triumph Motors will be celebrating its 110th anniversary in 2012. The three new models have boosted their line-up. This was officially unveiled at the EICMA show that happened in Milan, on November 8th. One of the new models is the Triumph Tiger Explorer, which will be coming into the market in 2012 is a flagship model. Already the Tiger 800 models, is a big hit in the European countries. Now, the Tiger Explorer which is powered by all new features is going to rock the market. The three cylinder engine which s shaft driven will deliver class leading performance. The adventure touring class is going to love it. The Tiger Explorer is the first one to use the ride-by-wire throttle system, and it also includes the cruise control system. The other high specification in this model includes the strong steel chassis, cast alloy wheels, stylish single-sided swing arm, and 19 inch front and 17 inch rear.

The iconic British motorcycle brand has announced its foray into the Indian market with its extensive range of premium models hitting the roads by next year. Triumph is one of the oldest motorcycle makers in the world and has been producing some of the finest machines for bikers all around the world. With international players entering the Indian market, Triumph too does not want to be left behind and has officially announced its entry into the country. For its Indian operations, Triumph has appointed Ashish Joshi as its Managing Director, who previously was heading the European operations for Royal Enfield and has a solid experience of sixteen years working in the in Asian and European markets. Commenting on Triumph's entry to the Indian market Nick Bloor, CEO of Triumph Motorcycles, UK said: "India is a very important motorcycle market and Triumph has assessed it carefully before deciding to step in. We see it as the next step in our global business model. The appointment of Ashish Joshi is a signal of our seriousness about success in India. I wish him the best and look forward to comprehensive growth from the Indian market."
